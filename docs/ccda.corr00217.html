Charles W. Chesnutt to Editor of The Journal of Negro History, 3 January 1917

CHAS. W. CHESNUTT

1105 WILLIAMSON BUILDING

CLEVELAND O

Cleveland, Ohio, January 3, 1917.



The Editor of The Journal of Negro History,

1219 You Street, N. W.,

Washington, D. C.



Dear Sir:- *

I am very glad to add my name to the list of subscribers to The Journal of Negro History. * It is so customary to assume that the Negro has contributed nothing noteworthy to civilization, that a magazine, which in the interst of truth, will call attention to the other side of the ledger, will supply a distinct need and ought to be cordially and adequately supported.



Sincerely yours,

Chas. W. Chesnutt.

CWC:MOC *

Notes:

Carter G. Woodson (19 December 1875-3 April 1950)

Carter G. Woodson was a professor and historian at Howard University. He was among the first black scholars to receive a doctoral degree and a pioneer of black history and African American studies. He founded The Journal of African American History (formally the Journal of Negro History), in January 1916 as one of the first scholarly texts or journals to cover African-American history. Woodson was the editor until 1950 (Korey Bowers Brown, “Carter G. Woodson,” Our History Association for the Study of African American Life and History, https://asalh.org/about-us/our-history/).

The Journal of Negro History

The Journal of Negro History was founded in 1916 by Dr. Carter G. Woodson, as one of the first journals to cover African American history. Though Chesnutt was consistently a subscriber to the journal, he did not contribute any publications.
