A DH Archival Project for ENGL878



Charles W. Chesnutt to Horace Traubel, 16 August 1902

1005 Williamson Building

Cleveland, O.

Aug 16, 1902.



Horace L. Traubel, Esq.,

Camden, N.J.



My dear Mr. Traubel:— *

I have been intending for sometime to put the "Conservator" * on the list of magazines which come to my house. It sounds a high clear note in favor of Truth and Justice and Civic Righteousness, which ought to be an doubtless is heard by many thousands, to whom I wish to be added. Will you kindly transmit the enclosed money order to the business management & request that the Conservator be sent to

"Chas. W. Chesnutt

64 Brenton St.

Cleveland, O."

Permit me to assure you still further of my full appreciation of the kind things you have said about my books &for your kindly interest in & support of the cause which they have been written to promote.



Sincerely yours,

Chas. W. Chesnutt.



Notes:

Horace Traubel (1858-1919)

Horace Traubel was an American poet, essayist, and author. Traubel was also a dedicated Socialist, and one of the founders of the socialist weekly newspaper The Worker. He is best known for being Whitman's literary executor and author of a nine-volume biography of hitman's final four years (1888-1892), entitled Walt Whitman in Camden.

This is one of the earliest letters that Chesnutt send Traubel. The two men had a friendly working relationship; Chesnutt was a fan of the magazine that Traubel founded, The Conservator, and Traubel consistently praised Chesnutt and his writing in reviews featured in his periodical.

The Conservator

The Conservator was a monthly newspaper founded and edited by Traubel for 30 years. The newspaper frequently reflected its editor's idiosyncratic philosophy and crusaded for libertarian principles.
