Charles W. Chesnutt to Horace Traubel, 19 December 1902

CHAS. W. CHESNUTT

1005 WILLIAMSON BUILDING

CLEVELAND, O.

December 19, 1902





My dear Mr. Traubel:— *

Thanks for your kind notice of "The Conjure Woman," * or rather of its author, in the November Conservator. *

Some of the plots of the stories—aside of course from their racial or sociological aspects, seem to have become popular of late. T. Anstey's * "The Brass Bottle" * has a professor who is changed by conjuration to a mule + indulges in antics very similar to those of the long–eared animal in "The Conjurer's Revenge," * while J.M. Barrie's * "Little White Bird * is an amplification of mark of^ the machinery of "Sis' Becky's Pickanninny." * These of course are probably mere coincidences. But they are interesting.

Thanking you again for your encouraging comment + appreciation, I remain



Cordially yours,

Chas. W. Chesnutt



Notes:

Horace Traubel (19 December 1858-9 September 1919)

Horace Traubel was an American poet, essayist, and author. Traubel was also a dedicated Socialist, and one of the founders of the socialist weekly newspaper The Worker. He is best known for being Whitman's literary executor and author of a nine-volume biography of Whitman's final four years (1888-1892), entitled Walt Whitman in Camden.

The Conjure Woman

The Conjure Woman is a collection of short stories by Chesnutt published in 1899. All of the tales are framed with narration by a white Northerner, John, who has travelled to the South with his wife, Annie, looking for a place to live. He recounts Uncle Julius, a man who was formerly enslaved on the land that the couple is looking to purchase, telling John and Annie conjure tales.

The Conservator

The Conservator was a monthly newspaper founded and edited by Traubel for 30 years. The newspaper frequently reflected its editor's idiosyncratic philosophy and crusaded for libertarian principles.



Notes:

Thomas Anstey Guthrie (8 August 1856-10 March 1943)

Thomas Anstey Guthrie was an English journalist and humor novelist known forVice Versa. He also wrote for stories for Punch magazine, including The Man from Blankley's, which was adapted into theatrical production.

The Brass Bottle

The Brass Bottle is a comedic novel by Thomas Anstey Guthrie, published under the pseudonym F. Anstey. The story follows a man who awakens a genie.

The Conjurer's Revenge

The Conjurer's Revenge is a short story by Chesnutt reprinted in The Conjure Woman after its initial publication in Overland Monthly. In this tale, Uncle Julius tells John and Annie about someone who was turned into a mule by the conjure woman. The story persuades John into purchasing a useless horse.



Sir James Matthew Barrie, 1st Baronet (9 May 1860-19 June 1937)

J.M. Barrie was a Scottish novelist and playwright best known for writing Peter Pan. George V made made Barrie a baronet in 1913 and a member of the Order of Merit in 1922.

Little White Bird

Little White Bird is a novel by J.M. Barrie, published in 1902 by Hodder & Stoughton and Scribner & Sons. It was also serialized in Scribner's magazine. The book introduces the character of Peter Pan and several of the chapters that include Pan were published separately as Peter Pan in Kensington Gardens.

Sis' Becky's Pickanninny

Sis' Becky's Pickanninnyis a conjure tale by Chesnutt featured in The Conjure Woman. The story takes place after John and Annie have moved South. John and Uncle Julius discuss Julius's rabbit's foot and other supersitious behaviors; Julius claims that if the main character of his story, Becky, had had a rabbit's foot, she wouldn't have watched her son and husband be sold into slavery.
