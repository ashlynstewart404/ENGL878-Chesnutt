Charles W. Chesnutt to Horace Traubel, 2 June 1905

CHAS. W. CHESNUTT

1005 WILLIAMSON BUILDING

CLEVELAND, O.

Cleveland,June 2,1905.



Mr. Horace L. Traubel,

Camden, N. J.



My Dear Traubel:— *

Your kind note asking me for a word to read on the occasion of the Walt Whitman Fellowship Meeting * on May 31st, came duly to hand. I did not reply promptly because I have been exceedingly busy for the past month, and had hoped to be present in New York at the date of the meeting, when I should have looked up the convention and said my word in person. This I was unfortunately not able to accomplish because of business reasons, and I was therefore not in the thing at all, except in spirit. I hope for better luck another time. What I look forward to with more pleasure than anything else, had I been able to be in New York, was the pleasure of meeting you, which I still hope to enjoy on some favorable occasion.



Yours very truly,

Chas. W. Chesnutt.



Notes:

Horace Traubel (19 December 1858-9 September 1919)

Horace Traubel was an American poet, essayist, and author. Traubel was also a dedicated Socialist, and one of the founders of the socialist weekly newspaper The Worker. He is best known for being Whitman's literary executor and author of a nine-volume biography of Whitman's final four years (1888-1892), entitled Walt Whitman in Camden.

Walt Whitman Fellowship: International

The Walt Whitman Fellowship: International was formally organized by Horace Traubel and several of Whitman's close friends in 1894, two years after Whitman's death, in order to ensure that Whitman's literary legacy would continue—and to celebrate his birthday each year. From 1895-1900 the group alternated their meeting place between Philadelphia and Boston. From 1901-1919, they met in New York, but other branches began to form in other cities across the United States. 123 issues of The Walt Whitman Papers were pubished, filled with several essays on Whitman and his work. The organization and the Fellowship Papers ended after Traubel's death in 1919.
